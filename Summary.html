SUMMARY


SERVER MANAGEMENT

Server management is the practice of maintaining, monitoring, and optimizing computer servers to ensure they operate efficiently, securely, and reliably. It involves tasks such as installing and updating operating systems, configuring hardware and software, managing user access and permissions, monitoring performance metrics, and implementing security measures like firewalls and regular patches. Administrators often use tools and platforms such as Microsoft Windows Server, Ubuntu Server, and VMware vSphere to manage physical and virtual environments. Effective server management also includes regular data backups, disaster recovery planning, and ensuring high availability to minimize downtime and protect critical business operations.

CYBER SECURITY

Cyber security is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, and damage. It involves implementing technologies, processes, and policies designed to safeguard sensitive information from threats such as malware, phishing, ransomware, and hacking attempts. Effective cyber security relies on tools like firewalls, encryption, antivirus software, and intrusion detection systems, as well as standards and frameworks such as National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) guidelines and the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) security standards. Organizations also depend on ethical hackers and security teams to identify vulnerabilities before malicious actors can exploit them. With the increasing reliance on digital systems, cyber security plays a critical role in protecting personal data, financial information, and national infrastructure.

CODING

Coding is the process of writing instructions that tell a computer how to perform specific tasks using programming languages like Python, Java, or C++. It involves breaking down problems into logical steps, designing algorithms, and translating those steps into precise syntax that a computer can understand and execute. Coders use tools such as text editors, integrated development environments (IDEs), and version control systems to build everything from websites and mobile apps to video games and artificial intelligence systems. Beyond just writing code, coding also includes testing, debugging, and improving programs to make them efficient, reliable, and user-friendly.

NETWORK SWITCH MANAGEMENT

Network switch management refers to the configuration, monitoring, and maintenance of network switches to ensure efficient, secure, and reliable data communication within an organization’s network. It involves tasks such as assigning IP addresses for remote access, configuring VLANs to segment traffic, setting up port security, enabling Quality of Service (QoS), and monitoring performance through protocols like SNMP. Managed switches from companies such as Cisco Systems, Juniper Networks, and Hewlett Packard Enterprise provide administrators with web-based interfaces, command-line access, or centralized management platforms to control traffic flow, enhance security, and troubleshoot issues. Effective switch management helps prevent network downtime, optimize bandwidth usage, and protect against unauthorized access or network attacks.
